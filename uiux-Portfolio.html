Project Overview

Project outline / Clients’ names / Project goals / Major deliverables

The goal:

increases the quality of medical data and shorten the working hours on annotation

Abstract:

The medical annotations represents a structured knowledge in an unstructured text. The task is done by feeding the annotations into a machine learning algorithm. It learns how to automatically extract the medical knowledge within the text. That medical data can be used to help the health professionals to improve the quality of care, to inhance a better patient experience, to decide more accurately and improve the healthcare outcomes.

NHIA (National Health Insurance Administration of Taiwan) was detected to create high-quality medical annotations. Traditionally, the annotation project managers (primary user) explained the data of the annotations and asked the annotators (secondary user) to take notes about the process and infrastructure of the medical annotation. However, many project managers did not give a comprehensive description of the project. The annotation of data was tend to be inefficient and incorrect. Consequently, the team members needed more time to go over all the data which could not be understood and used by the *NLP models.

During the first stage of the annotation software design, the team focused on the survey of the needs of the medical annotation projects, which includes the medical content, the process, the infrastructure, and the logics of input. During the later stage, the team concentrated on the process of how to use the UI interfaces and passing the usability tests. Most of the project managers were not familiar with the computers, so the interface was designed to be simple and intuitive. The users could get their tasks done easily with minimum effort.

The primary user of Anota indicated that this software gave a huge support on improving the data quality and reducing the working hours on annotation. Anota is going to be released online in the coming future.

Starting the Design

Includes problems, solutions, flowcharts, wireframes to see the begin of the design

The goal:

Empowering the annotation project managers (primary user) to create a medical annotation project schedule with clear plans, process and efficiency delegating tasks to annotators through an easy to access, understood, and used software. By doing so, the annotators could see the big picture of the annotation projects to get more work done, more efficiently

Traditionally, most of the project managers only provided annotation files to annotators, but did not explain the project gaol, related medical knowledge, annotation terms, each step and details of the annotation process. Mostly, the project managers gave verbal unstructured information about the annotation process and steps or gave nothing to the annotators, which made the annotators feel confused and did not know how to complete the tasks.

Therefore, the team focused on digging out the insights on stakeholders of the plan and feedback (questions, concerns, or ideas) towards annotation projects. To help the annotation teams be able to launch projects faster and more efficiently.

Solutions:

To let the project managers give a clear, comprehended picture of the projects as well as attached all of the needed files. The project managers only needed to follow the steps on UI. They will have a clear infrastructure that outlines the project’s schedule and steps.

Progress in order:

To give the users distinct steps in annotating, the team provided a full description of how to create annotation projects to help the user have logical steps in planning annotation projects. The project managers only had to follow the step to complete the task in order.

Easy-to-use graphical interface:

the users could use Anota without having to know commands. The users just needed to interact with the interface and followed the elements and objects to define the annotation project.

A well-defined and well-executed UX process

The online portfolio does not require any kind of tutorials or tooltips, users will know how to use it and complete their tasks in no time.

Consistency and Standards in User Interface Design

To eliminate confusion and shorten the length of the thought process for users while navigating through this unfamiliar portfolio. Consistency and standards were applied when designing the user interface to help them maintain consistency and cohesion across the product portfolio. To put it more clearly, the indicator is divided into the following parts:

  • colour
  • fonts
  • shape
  • size

Provide concise stories

In order to explain the project more clearly and concisely, a STAR interview response method was used to describe the context. Those are:

  • Situation: Describe the context in which the author does work or faces work challenges
  • Task: Describe the author's responsibilities in that situation
  • Action: Describe how the author accomplished the task or worked hard to meet the challenge
  • Result: Explain the result or result of the action taken.

More details about typography

Both good and bad typography has to do with the reading experience. Therefore, more indicators are set to judge the typography of the portfolio:

  • Images: Images should convey meaningful information
  • Iconography: Applying iconography to capture the user's attention and transition to another location
  • Spacing: To improve readability, typography must also be visually interesting and consistent

Flowchart:

To let project managers comprehend how to design annotation projects instantly, the flowchart was emphasised to have a clear description of the planning process for annotation projects. Under the circumstances, it allowed project managers not only to have a logical start and end, but also to understand and follow the steps easily when they used them.

Wireframe:

During wireframing phase, the idea placing all of the flow on one screen was also suggested to the primary users, so that the users could work everything on one screen without too much effort. However, the users indicated that they were not used to using a computer screen which there were too many selections and steps on. They would feel confused and contradicted. It would take them more time to complete the project.

Refining the design

utilise visual design to help the users to access, and understand the user interface

UI Design:

This UI aimed to facilitate the seamless achievement of the task at hand and was aesthetically enjoyable for the users to operate. Therefore, keeping the UI clear and concise was a priority. Besides, to give the users a glimpse into what the process requires, a progress step was designed to make the users know where they are in the process.

Colour:

The colour blue was typically connected with calm and focus. Therefore, colour blue was chosen to keep the users’ mental focus in building annotation projects.

Typography:

The team expected that the users could read the text without effort and also made good use of the information the team wanted to convey. Therefore, the most crucial requirement for fonts was that they were legible. Japanese sans-serif was applied in Chinese, whiled Calibri was in English because of their readability and legibility in Web and mobile user-interfaces.

User Feedback:

The users gave great praise to Anota, saying it helped them not only managed annotation projects efficiently, but also increased about 2% of the quality of annotation. Due to covid-19, the users had to work remotely. Thus, they hoped the application would be online in order to use everywhere.

Limitation & Reflection:

The contract with NHIA pointed out the team were only allowed to have interviews and testing with a few of the project managers. Most of the insights, however, should be provided from annotators, not from their supervisors (project managers). That is, when the annotators faced precise problems, there was no way for us to get the whole vision of the complete scenario. As the result, the challenges, questions, feedback and assumptions from the real users (project managers) might remain error.
