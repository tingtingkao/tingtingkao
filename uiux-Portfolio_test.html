Project Overview

Project outline / Clients’ names / Project goals / Major deliverables

Objective:

Improve the user experience of Ting-Ting's (author) online portfolio to increase the likelihood of being hired for a Senior UI/UX Designer position

Challenge:

Demonstrate the author's skills, UI/UX work experience and previous projects to build a strong personal brand on the portfolio

Overview:

For more recruitment opportunities for senior UI/UX designer positions, I developed an online portfolio with CSS and Html. Two months later, I got a lot of interview opportunities. However, these opportunities are not as ideal as I imagined. After reviewing resumes, portfolios, and career plans, I found that I needed to redesign the portfolio because most interviewers gave negative feedback on the user experience of the portfolio. Also, the previous portfolio was more about product planning than optimizing the user experience. After gathering input from interviewers and designers, the questions were identified:

1. The previous portfolio was designed more like a game but ignored the scene; the interviewer only had a limited time to learn about the designer's skills and work experience in UI/UX.

2. The previous portfolio text did not provide clear text guidelines, causing the interviewer to ignore the entire project. They cannot immediately see the author's structure, logic, and patterns in the text.

3. The text of the portfolio should focus more on how the author designs a better user experience and the decision-making process for the user interface, rather than focusing too much on describing the process of developing the product.

To address these issues, an interviewer persona was first created to describe the problems and scenarios that interviewers face during the talent recruitment process. Second, metrics to measure success and identify key performance are set and can be used for discussions with other designers. Third, in order to make the text of the portfolio meet the interviewer's criteria, the portfolio of six designers and the portfolio of four design studios are used as the outline of the portfolio. Finally, apply *STAR to the text, describing the situation the author encountered, the task that had to be dealt with, the action is taken, and the result the author got.

*What Is the STAR Interview Response Method?

The STAR interview response method is a way of answering behavioural interview questions. Behavioural interview questions are questions about how you have behaved in the past. Specifically, they are about how you have handled certain work situations. Employers using this technique analyse jobs and define the skills and qualities that high-level performers have exhibited in that job.

He is only willing to spend time on candidates whose skills, work experience and contributions are suitable for the position. Otherwise, he just gave a quick review. he also uses recruiting software and has professional support from recruiters. However, it is still difficult to find a candidate who not only has good work experience but also has a good cultural fit on the team. Sometimes, he uses his tablet and smartphone to view her portfolio. For him, a good portfolio should give a logical, clear story and present an aesthetically pleasing interface. Additionally, the clarity and consistency of the portfolio's text and the interface were important to him.

Starting the Design

Includes problems, solutions, flowcharts, wireframes to understand the beginning of a design

Problem:

How to improve the user experience of the author's portfolio and let users know the author's design ability and work experience in UI/UX

Solutions:

After speaking with people who provide interviews for job applicants, an outline is organized to evaluate and review the authors' portfolios. A metric is also used to discuss with experienced designers. The indicator is divided into two parts, the text of the portfolio and the user interface. User experience design is divided into mobile and website user experiences to meet the unique needs and criteria of users.

Establish Brand Identity in the User Interface:

A user's first impression usually comes from a reaction to a visual feature. In order to help users understand the author's personality, design ability, and visual storytelling skills from the portfolio, the indicators are divided into the following sections:

1. Visual attractive 2. Visual intrigue

Know Where to Focus First and What Action to Take:

Implement visual hierarchy in the design, allowing users to find the information they need immediately without being confused and overwhelmed by information

Refining the design

Utilize visual design to help users access and understand user interfaces

UI Design:

The initial portfolio focused on developing a gamified website that would allow users to have a unique experience and be more interactive with the portfolio. However, due to some technical issues and the user experience of the previous product portfolio is not in line with the user's habits. Therefore, the product portfolio is focused on creating a good experience for the user. In terms of visual interface design, focus on showing the author's personality, life experience and the growth process as a designer.

Colour:

The first step in defining colour is to choose a primary colour. The colour has high value and low saturation. The colour blue gives users a calming and relaxing effect, allowing them to feel more visual comfort. It also provides users with a unique identity of the author's personality and personal brand.

Typography:

Google open source font families were applied in the text of the portfolio. Furthermore, to give the users a distinct visual impression of key visuals. An own typeface was designed based on the font Bellona as well as combined the author’s experience in writing Western calligraphy.

Layout:

In the beginning, the words in the portfolio were not arranged artistically. Users can't find exactly what they're looking for, and it's hard to jump to the areas they needed more than the others. However, the text was rearranged and the following typography tricks were set:

1. The text in the folio is divided into five main sections, and users can easily understand the author's structure, logic, and patterns in the folio. By doing this, the user can view the entire project as the author has in mind.

2. Use icons to help users easily identify elements with the same function and differences identified in the main and subtitles.

3. Text chunking is supported, using chunking to affect how text is read. This helps users to easily distinguish the differences between different groups and texts.
